Terrain-Aware Global Path Planning for Planetary Vehicles with Moving Direction Constraints

Overview

Global path planning serves as a critical foundation for planetary vehicles to carry out long-distance exploration missions. When navigating through soft and unstructured extraterrestrial environments, planetary vehicles encounter numerous challenges, with significant risks including wheel slippage and sideslip. These risks are not only contingent upon the geometry and mechanical properties of the terrain but are also influenced by the vehicle's movement behavior. To accurately evaluate the traversability of extensive terrains and chart a path that is both safe and efficient for planetary rovers, this paper proposes a novel global path planning method. This method is based on an explicit wheel-soil interaction model, which integrates the terrain geometry, soil mechanical properties, and vehicle's driving directions to construct a multi-layered traversability map. The proposed approach also features online replanning capabilities, enabling the updating of the traversability map under conditions of limited data, thus ensuring the flexibility and responsiveness of path planning.

Methodology

The global path planning method proposed in this study is divided into two primary phases: offline global path planning and online path replanning. During the offline planning phase, the method estimates potential wheel slip ratio and sideslip angle by integrating three critical components—the terrain surface normal vector, the wheel-soil interaction model, and the vehicle's driving directions. These elements contribute to the assessment of terrain traversability, and lead to the generation of a precomputed traversability map, which serves as the foundation for subsequent global path planning. This phase leverages detailed analysis to predict vehicle behavior across diverse terrains, ensuring that initial paths are optimized for safety and efficiency. In the online phase, the method addresses the inherent uncertainties in the initial parameter estimates of the ABTM by continuously acquiring data during the planetary vehicle's traversal. Online parameter estimation and high-slip detection are integrated in this method, facilitating dynamic refinement of the traversability map. Consequently, the global path planning remains responsive to actual driving conditions and environmental variations, enabling immediate adjustments that enhance the vehicle's adaptability in complex terrains.

Simulation Experiments

Quantitative Results

Extensive validation through high-fidelity simulations have demonstrated superior comprehensive performance of the proposed method compared to existing methods, especially in terms of path execution success rates and execution efficiency.

  • Solved rate (Sol.) [%] is defined as the percentage of test instances for which a feasible path is successfully generated.
  • Computation time (tcomput) [s] includes the time for terrain traversability map calculation and the time for the path search process.
  • Average path length (Lmean) [m] represents the mean length of all successfully planned paths.
  • Success rate (Succ.) [%] refers to the proportion of attempts that successfully reached the goal of the rover when following the planned paths.
  • Execution time (texecut) [s] expresses the actual traversal time along the successfully executed paths.
